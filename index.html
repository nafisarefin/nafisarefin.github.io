Hello.

I am a final year PhD candidate in Biomedical Engineering at the University of Tennessee.

My research focuses on tracking emboli throughout the cardiovascular system — from the brain to the abdominal organs — including the Circle of Willis, thoracic aorta, and abdominal aorta. Through these simulations, I study hemodynamic patterns and assess disease risks such as stroke, acute kidney injury, and hepatic complications.

I have a combined background in computational modeling (CFD, FSI, LPT, and image-based modeling using CT/MRI) and experimental methods such as blood pump trials and PIV-based flow validation.

Before joining UTK, I completed my M.S. in Mechanical Engineering from UTS, Australia, and my B.S. in Aerospace Engineering from IIU, Malaysia. I grew up in Dhaka, Bangladesh.
