Research

My research bridges computational fluid dynamics (CFD) and machine learning (ML) to improve biomedical simulations and device modeling. Projects include:

Emboli Transport and Hemodynamics in Cardiovascular Systems

Developed patient-specific CFD-LPT models to simulate embolic trajectories in cerebral, thoracic, and abdominal arteries. This research evaluates stroke and organ injury risks under varied cannula placement and CPB flow parameters.

Stochastic Compact Modeling of Emerging Semiconductor Devices

Designed ML-based Quantile Mixture Density Network (QMDN) architectures to capture cycle-to-cycle and device-to-device variability in FeFET I–V characteristics. Enabled distribution-aware circuit simulations and inverse design through quantile sampling.

Multiphysics Modeling of Blood Pumps

Integrated fluid–structure interaction (FSI) with in vitro PIV data to validate CFD simulations of centrifugal and axial blood pumps. Investigated shear stress and flow stagnation zones for thrombogenic risk assessments.

Image-Based Modeling from Medical Scans

Processed CT/MRI images to reconstruct vascular anatomies for simulation-ready 3D models. Applied this workflow to cerebral and aortic regions in pediatric and adult patients.
